Vegetable Soup

Description

Vegetable soup is a comforting and hearty dish consisting of a variety of vegetables.

The foundation of the soup is typically a vegetable or chicken broth. This liquid is what carries and amplifies the flavors of all the ingredients. Some recipes may call for a tomato-based broth, while others are clear.

Ingredients

  • Vegetables
  • Liquid Base
  • Aromatics
  • Seasonings
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Prepare Your Vegetables:
    • Wash and chop all your chosen vegetables (carrots, celery, onions, potatoes, etc.) into bite-sized pieces. Mince the garlic.
  2. Sauté Aromatics:
    • Heat a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
    • Add a small amount of cooking oil (like olive oil).
    • Add the chopped onions and celery to the pot and sauté for 5-7 minutes until they soften and become translucent.
    • Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.
  3. Add Remaining Vegetables and Broth:
    • Add the chopped carrots and potatoes to the pot and stir for a couple of minutes.
    • Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth. Make sure the liquid covers all the vegetables.
  4. Simmer the Soup:
    • Bring the soup to a boil over medium-high heat.
    • Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until all the vegetables are tender when pierced with a fork.
  5. Season and Serve:
    • Taste the soup and season with salt and black pepper to your liking.
    • Serve hot, optionally garnished with fresh herbs if you have them.
